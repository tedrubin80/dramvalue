# DramValue — Product Context

## Product Purpose

DramValue is a whisky price intelligence platform for consumers who want to buy smart. It aggregates auction results and retail prices across 10+ sources, giving anyone access to the kind of market knowledge that usually lives only in the head of a seasoned collector.

## Register

product

## Users

**Primary**: Whisky enthusiasts who buy bottles — collectors, casual buyers, gift-buyers. They range from occasional drinkers researching a purchase to serious collectors tracking auction trends. Most are not experts; they just don't want to get ripped off.

**Secondary**: Bar owners and retailers benchmarking their inventory against market prices.

## Brand Voice

The knowledgeable drinking buddy. The friend at the bar who's had the Yamazaki 12 and the Pappy and isn't impressed by either label — he knows what's worth the money. Warm, direct, occasionally funny. Never condescending. Never using "premium" or "luxury" without irony. He calls a $40 bottle that punches above its weight a better deal than a $400 bottle that doesn't.

Anti-references (do NOT sound like these):
- Wine Spectator / Robert Parker: pretentious, score-obsessed, gatekeeping
- Financial data terminals: cold, dense, jargon-heavy
- Generic SaaS dashboards: clinical, faceless, "insights-driven"
- Whisky brand marketing: faux-artisan, smoke-and-oak clichés

## Tone Principles

- Specific over vague ("sold for £180 at Whisky Auctioneer in March" beats "recently sold")
- The data is the feature — let it speak; don't editorialize around it
- Friendly friction: warn when something seems overpriced; celebrate when something is a steal
- Never patronizing about budget or taste

## Anti-Patterns for This Product

- Luxury aesthetics (dark velvet, gold, serif headers, moody whisky photography as texture)
- Gamification badges or streak counters
- "Discover your taste profile" questionnaires
- Any copy that positions whisky as aspirational / exclusive

## Strategic Principles

- Price transparency is the product; never hide the data behind friction
- Mobile-first: buyers are looking this up at a shop or auction on their phone
- Speed matters more than polish: someone checking a price before bidding doesn't wait
